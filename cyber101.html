What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and programs from digital attacks. It is crucial for protecting personal information, business secrets, and national security. The importance of cybersecurity has grown with the increasing amount of sensitive data stored online. From the early days of computing to today's sophisticated defenses, cybersecurity has evolved to meet the growing threats posed by hackers and cyber criminals. Effective cybersecurity measures can prevent data breaches and ensure the smooth operation of essential services.

What is Cyber Crime?

Cyber crime refers to criminal activities carried out using computers or the internet. These can include identity theft, phishing schemes, and hacking to steal sensitive information. It also encompasses activities like spreading malware or conducting ransomware attacks to extort money from victims. Understanding cyber crime is essential for developing strategies to protect individuals and organizations from these threats.
